Where to go

Control catalog · Rev 5

NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5

The catalog of security & privacy controls you tailor and select in the RMF's Select step. Published September 2020 — technology-neutral, and now applies beyond just federal IT. Two families (PT, SR) were new in Rev 5, bringing the total to 20.

20 control families Low baseline: 150 controls Moderate: 304 controls High: 392 controls

Companion doc: 800-53A provides the assessment procedures used to test these controls in the Assess step. NIST also released Rev 5.2 (2025) adding software-resiliency controls in response to EO 14306.

NIST AI 100-1

AI Risk Management Framework

Voluntary, non-certifiable guidance for managing risk across an AI system's lifecycle — released Jan 26, 2023. Still the current version; NIST extends it through profiles rather than new version numbers.

Companion profile: NIST AI 600-1 (Generative AI Profile, July 2024) applies these four functions to generative-AI-specific risks.

Final v2.0 · Feb 2024

Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0

A broader, sector-agnostic risk framework — not RMF-specific — organized around six functions. Govern was added in 2.0 as the cross-cutting function that directs the other five.

Two overlapping vocabularies

Legacy (8570) vs. current (8140.03)

DoD 8570.01-M was officially cancelled Feb 15, 2023 and replaced by DoDM 8140.03, which uses the DoD Cyber Workforce Framework (DCWF) — 74 specific work roles at Basic/Intermediate/Advanced proficiency, instead of the old IAT/IAM/IASAE + Level I-III system. In practice, contracts and job postings still use the legacy IAT/IAM/IASAE language below because contractor policy (DFARS) hasn't fully caught up — so both vocabularies matter day to day.
